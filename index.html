About

Until May 2019 I was a research associate in semantics/pragmatics at the University of Potsdam. In 2017, I joined the research group Exhaustiveness in embedded questions (ExQ), led by Malte Zimmermann (Potsdam) and Edgar Onea (Graz). The group, which is part of the XPrag.de priority program, investigates the exhaustivity properties of questions from an experimental and cross-linguistic perspective. My prior work looked at the expression of definiteness across languages, with a particular focus on Akan – a Kwa language spoken primarily in Ghana. I am also interested in demonstratives and how they are expressed in Spanish and other Romance languages.
